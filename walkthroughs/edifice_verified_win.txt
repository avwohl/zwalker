# The Edifice (1st place, 1997 IF Competition; XYZZY Awards for Best Puzzles
# and Best Individual Puzzle) is an "interactive allegory" of human
# prehistory: a mysterious tower appears in a proto-human's forest, and each
# level's east door opens onto a formative moment of our species. The game is
# scoreless - SCORE prints prose ("You have visited N levels of the Edifice
# and solved M of them") - so this walkthrough carries a WIN_TEXT directive.
#
# The route solves all three implemented levels:
#   Level 1 (Toolmaking and Fire): knock a dead branch off the tree with the
#     rock, strip and snap it into a stick and a log, whittle the stick into
#     a spear, practice on the stump until "quite skilled" (13 throws), kill
#     Beast the moment it grazes nearby, carve meat with the rock, then
#     strike rock on the flat rock to spark twigs into a fire, feed it
#     branches and the log, and eat the cooked meat ("strangely content").
#   Level 2 (Language, the famous puzzle): your son is dying of fever and
#     only Feverleaf can save him. Make a drawing kit (bone from the firepit,
#     white bark from the river trees, berries smashed into purple paste),
#     draw Feverleaf on the bark, then learn the stranger's tongue by
#     pointing at things and repeating his words back ("na"/"fa" = I/you,
#     "salum" = hut, "scarmul" = knife, "rema" = son, "ben" = is,
#     "akne" = sick). Cough, tell him "ne rema ben akne" (my son is sick),
#     and show him the bark: "A! Rakasha!" - he fetches the herb himself.
#     Carrying Feverleaf into your hut triggers the all-night vigil that
#     saves your son and seals the friendship.
#   Level 3 (Horse Riding): the village fears Marauders. Lure a horse from
#     the box canyon with a piece of fruit, drop the fruit below the ridge
#     cliff and jump onto the horse's back while it eats. Ride out the
#     bucking: lean into each veer, pull the mane to force it left around
#     the boulder, then use the freed hand to cram your feather headdress
#     over its eyes - the blinded horse calms, and the whole village takes
#     up riding, ready to face the Marauders.
# After the third solve the Edifice rumbles and grows a new floor; climbing
# past Level Four's etchings of wandering tribes ends the game.
