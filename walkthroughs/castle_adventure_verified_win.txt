# Ending: the game's best ("super happy") ending, reached in 277 moves. A
# knight crosses a forest, shrinks himself with a mushroom to squeeze into a
# cave, robs a skeleton of its gold key, opens the caretaker's hut, and rides
# the secret well down under the moat. Inside the castle he feeds the guard
# dog a sausage (thrown south into the pit), calms a ghost by forcing the
# panel above the royal bed and finding her ring, uses the ring to open the
# cellar floor, crosses the labyrinth by the tapestry codes (esw / ene / wns),
# drugs the dungeon guard with a bottle of wine, and frees the princess.
# When a ghost spooks her on the way out, her dropped tiara lures the ghost
# away; the princess is recovered on the highest turret, the drawbridge winch
# is kicked (her idea), and the pair walk out north. Because the shiny gold
# key was left out for the magpie after unlocking the hut (instead of being
# hidden in the pouch), the guard dog Fido vouches for you before the king,
# and "you marry the princess and live happily ever after, all because you
# showed kindness to a pesky magpie!" followed by *** The End ***.
#
# Why a pinned seed: the game's one true RNG puzzle is the pitch-dark cellar
# under the caretaker's hut - each GET FLASHLIGHT is a random grope (the
# game's own hints say "Just keep trying!"). Under seed 1 the flashlight
# turns up on the 4th try; the list pads eight attempts so the recording is
# stable ("You already have that." afterwards is harmless). Everything else
# that looks random is turn-scheduled and replays identically: the magpie
# swoops, the mushroom shrink timer, the ghost appearances, and the dungeon
# guard's patrol (guard visible from behind the barrels on arrival and every
# 5th turn after; the bottle of wine is planted two turns after a sighting
# and he drinks it on his next pass).
#
# Release note: this is Release 2 (serial 121207), which renamed Release 1's
# "electric torch" to "flashlight" - the route was machine-realigned for
# that. The guardhouse door is unlocked BEFORE walking into it: bumping a
# locked door costs nothing in-game, but the replay pathway rolls back
# blocked moves and the rollback swallows the following command, so the
# route avoids blocked moves entirely.
#

# walkthrough carries a WIN_TEXT directive matching the super-happy-ending
